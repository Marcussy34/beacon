# Introduction

**Beacon is mission control for your AI coding agents.** It lives in your macOS menu bar and watches
every **Claude Code** and **Codex CLI** session running across all your repos at once.

## The problem

If you run more than a couple of AI coding agents at the same time, you know the tax: a `claude` here,
three `codex` sessions there, scattered across terminal tabs, VS Code, and Cursor windows. One is waiting
on a permission prompt. Another finished two minutes ago. **Which one?** You end up cycling through windows
hunting for the agent that needs you.

Beacon answers that question at a glance. It badges your menu bar the moment any session needs input or
finishes, and a single keystroke (**⌘⇧Space**) opens a floating panel showing every session and its live
state. Click **Go to** and Beacon snaps you to the exact terminal tab or editor window.

## What you get

- **All your agents, one glance** — live state for every Claude Code and Codex session: `needs-you`,
  `working`, `done`, and `recently closed`.
- **Ambient menu-bar badge** — the icon lights up with a count the instant a session needs you. No need
  to summon anything.
- **A floating ⌘⇧Space panel** — a persistent HUD that floats above everything and follows you across
  Spaces and displays.
- **"Go to" that lands** — focuses the right window, and on supported setups the exact integrated-terminal
  tab.
- **Zero per-repo setup** — one global hook install covers every repo, for both tools.
